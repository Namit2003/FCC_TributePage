Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

"Dream is not the thing you see in sleep but is that thing that doesn't let you sleep." ~ APJ Abdul Kalam.

Here's a time line of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam life:

  • 15th October 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1954: Graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli with a degree in Physics
  • 1955: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist
  • 1960s: Worked on the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and ballistic missile (Agni)
  • 1982-1992: Served as the Director of DRDO
  • 1992-1997: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India
  • 1998-2001: Served as the Chairman of the DRDO and the Secretary of the Department of Defence Research and Development
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India
  • 27th July 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong, Meghalaya

Throughout his life, APJ Abdul Kalam was known for his contributions to India's space and defense programs, as well as for his commitment to education and youth empowerment. He was a beloved figure in India and around the world, and his legacy continues to inspire people to this day.
